OPERATION HUNTER KILLER — HOW THE GAME WORKS
================================================
Written for Tim, so we always mean the same thing.
Also useful for Claude AI to read at the start of a session.


THE BASIC IDEA
--------------
You control a submarine hunting an enemy submarine through a 3D version
of your home. The rooms, walls and furniture of your house become the
battlefield. Surface ships patrol above the water and drop depth charges
on you. You fire torpedoes, use sonar, and try to survive.

The game runs in a web browser. It is deployed at hunter-killer.vercel.app


THE TWO MAIN VIEWS
------------------

THE COMMAND VIEW
  What you see: A bird's-eye view of the whole battlefield, slightly
  angled like looking at a model from above. You can see walls, rooms,
  both submarines, and the terrain. You can rotate and zoom this view.
  Tim calls it: "the command view", "the map", "top-down"
  Code calls it: viewMode = 'command'

THE PERISCOPE VIEW (the main game view)
  What you see: First-person view from inside the water, looking around
  at eye level. You can drag left/right to look around, up/down to look
  above or below you. The terrain walls and floor glow around you.
  This is where you actually play the game — navigate, fire torpedoes,
  and dodge depth charges.
  Tim calls it: "the underwater view", "the periscope view"
  Code calls it: viewMode = 'periscope'

These two views are the important ones. There are two more that happen
automatically when you reach the surface:

THE SURFACE VIEW
  Happens automatically when your sub rises near the top of the water.
  You see the sky and clouds, the water surface below you, and ships
  on the horizon as silhouettes. You can rotate the view horizontally.
  Tim calls it: "the surface view", "the view with the clouds",
               "where you can see the ships"
  Code calls it: viewMode = 'surface'

THE SURFACED VIEW
  Happens when your sub fully breaks the surface. You see a submarine
  deck image. You are now sitting on top of the water.
  Code calls it: viewMode = 'surfaced'


THE WORLD
---------
The game world is a big 3D box. Think of it like a fish tank:

  TOP = the water surface. Ships float here. This is where Y = GRID.H.
  BOTTOM = the seabed. Terrain mountains sit on this. This is Y = 0.
  Your submarine moves up and down between the seabed and the surface.

For a normal house map, the box is quite shallow (like a room, 3 metres tall).
For the canyon/terrain map, the box is very deep (like an underwater canyon).


THE TERRAIN TYPES
-----------------
There are two kinds of battleground:

HOUSE / FLOOR PLAN MAPS
  Built from a photo or drawing of a floor plan. You see rooms, walls,
  corridors and furniture. The world is relatively shallow.
  Examples: your house maps.

CANYON / TERRAIN MAP
  Built from a grayscale image where bright = tall mountain, dark = deep
  valley. You get an underwater canyon landscape with peaks and ravines.
  Example: "THE CANYON" battleground.


THE SONAR (the small circle map, bottom left of screen)
-------------------------------------------------------
Always visible no matter which view you are in. Shows a top-down map of
the battlefield. Updates every frame.

On house maps: shows room outlines and furniture as faint rectangles.
On canyon map: shows a topographic contour map — dark colours at the
  deep seabed, bright teal at the mountain peaks, with contour lines
  between the height bands (like an old-fashioned map).

Also shows: your position (green dot), enemy position (red, only when
revealed), torpedoes (orange streaks), ships (gold diamonds).


THE SHIPS
---------
Surface ships (battleships) patrol the top of the water. They:
  - Are visible in the SURFACE VIEW as full battleship silhouettes on
    the horizon (with gun turrets, bridge, funnels, masts)
  - Are shown in the COMMAND VIEW from above as a top-down ship shape
  - Are NOT visible in the underwater periscope view (too distracting)
  - Show as gold diamond blips on the sonar
  - Drop DEPTH CHARGES when you are in Battle Stations mode (red alert)


DEPTH CHARGES
-------------
Dropped by ships when you press RED ALERT and a ship is above you.
They fall slowly through the water (about 7 seconds to reach the bottom),
look like small barrels, leave an orange dot trail behind them, and
glow red when they are close to your depth. They explode and damage
your hull if they get close.


THE BUTTONS / CONTROLS
----------------------
COMMAND VIEW controls (bottom of screen):
  PING = send out a sonar ping to reveal the enemy
  HIDE ENEMY / SHOW ENEMY = toggle enemy visibility
  PERISCOPE = switch to the periscope view
  TORPEDO = arm a torpedo
  FIRE = launch the torpedo
  VIEW = reset the camera angle
  RESPAWN = move your sub back to the start

PERISCOPE VIEW controls (on-screen zones):
  Left drag zone = move your sub and aim
  Right drag zone = rise or dive
  FIRE button = launch torpedo
  COMMAND button = go back to the command view
  PING button = sonar ping
  RED ALERT = battle stations (ships start dropping depth charges)


THE DOT CLOUD
-------------
The game world is covered in thousands of tiny glowing dots on every
surface. This gives the game its "sonar scan" look. The dots are
coloured by what surface they sit on:
  Terrain/mountains = teal to bright cyan (darker at seabed, brighter
    at peaks) — same colours as the sonar contour map
  Walls = blue
  Furniture = warm amber/gold

You can make the dots bigger or smaller with the circle +/- buttons.

DEPLOYMENT
----------
The game is live at: hunter-killer.vercel.app
To deploy new changes: open a terminal in the project folder and run
  git push
or
  npx vercel --prod


FILE LOCATIONS
--------------
The game code is almost entirely in one file:
  src/game.js  (this is where almost everything lives, about 6700 lines)
  index.html   (the page layout and buttons)
  src/style.css  (colours and styling)
