MISSION BRIEFING
You command a nuclear submarine in hostile waters. Your objective: locate and destroy the enemy Typhoon-class submarine BRAVO before it destroys you.

Navigate through ocean trenches, floor plans, and volcanic battlegrounds. Use stealth, sonar, and superior tactics to gain the upper hand. Every kill earns points — but one mistake can implode your hull.

VIEW MODES
Your submarine has four operational views. Switch between them using the PERISCOPE and COMMAND buttons, and by adjusting depth.
PERISCOPE
3D first-person underwater view. See terrain, the enemy sub, torpedoes, and all sea creatures. Your primary combat view.
SURFACE
Rise to periscope depth. The ocean surface appears above you. Ships become visible — they will fire on you.
SURFACED
Break fully through the surface. Your deck is visible. Hull repairs automatically. Ships are extremely dangerous here.
COMMAND MAP
Top-down tactical map. Plan your approach, see waypoints, and review the battlefield. Press COMMAND to enter, PERISCOPE to return.
CONTROLS

NAVIGATION & SONAR
You have three navigation tools. Use them together to build a complete picture of the battlefield.
MINIMAP (Left Panel)
Shows your position, enemy contacts, torpedo tracks, waypoints, and surface ship positions. In ocean terrain mode it renders a sonar contour map of the seabed. Also your movement control area — drag here to move.
DEPTH GAUGE (Right Panel)
Vertical track showing your depth (A marker) and the enemy's depth (B marker, red). The waterline is at the top. The crush depth zone grows in red as your hull weakens — descend into it at your peril.
SONAR SWEEP
The rotating green sweep on the minimap reveals contacts. In SILENT RUNNING mode, fire a silent ping with the PING button to briefly light up the terrain without making noise.
COMPASS
The compass strip at the top shows your bearing in degrees. Drag it to turn. In command view the compass rotates the camera.
WEAPONS
You have two torpedo types. Both fire from your current position in your facing direction. Time your shots — torpedoes travel fast but run out of fuel.
⊙ STANDARD TORPEDO
Press FIRE to launch. Travels straight ahead at high speed. Will detonate on contact with the enemy sub or any solid obstacle. Two direct hits destroy BRAVO. Three hits when their hull is reinforced.
◈ ACOUSTIC TORPEDO
Press ACOUSTIC to launch a homing torpedo. It locks onto the enemy's acoustic signature and steers toward them — but only if you have an acoustic lock (keep the enemy centred in your crosshairs to build lock). Can be spoofed by countermeasures.
⊛ NOISEMAKER
Press NOISE to deploy a decoy barrel. It emits a loud acoustic signal that diverts incoming homing torpedoes. Use it when BRAVO fires on you. Limited supply.
THREATS
Multiple threats will target you. Learn to recognise each and respond accordingly.
ENEMY TORPEDO
BRAVO fires standard and acoustic torpedoes. You will hear a sonar ping warning. Evade by moving perpendicular to the incoming track, or deploy a noisemaker to redirect homing rounds.
DEPTH CHARGES
Surface ships drop depth charges when you are detected. They sink slowly and detonate at depth — stay deep and move. The closer the blast, the more hull damage you take. Reduce your detection score by going silent.
SURFACE SHIPS
Warships patrol the surface. If you surface or go to periscope depth, they may detect you. At BATTLE STATIONS they become very aggressive. Naval gun shells arc toward your position.
SEA CREATURES
Whales, megalodons, and giant squid inhabit the deep. They are not hostile, but their sonar signatures can mask the enemy sub — or startle you at the worst moment.
STEALTH & ALERT STATES
Managing your noise signature and alert state is critical to survival.
SILENT RUNNING
Press SILENT to cut all active sonar and reduce engine noise. The enemy cannot detect you as easily and loses track faster. Movement speed is halved. In silent mode, use the PING button to fire a single passive ping that briefly reveals terrain without broadcasting your position.
BATTLE STATIONS
Press the RED ALERT image button below your lives panel to toggle battle stations. At battle stations, surface ships become highly aggressive and your detection threshold rises. Use it when you want to go offensive and accept the increased surface threat. The alert image turns red when active.
DETECTION METER
The DETECT bar shows how well the enemy has found you. High detection means they will fire more often and ships will ping more aggressively. Stay deep, go silent, and use terrain for cover to reduce detection.
HULL & STATUS PANEL
Monitor your submarine's health at all times. Ignoring the hull gauge is how commanders die.
HULL INTEGRITY
Shown as a percentage in the status panel. Every torpedo hit, depth charge, and collision reduces it. At 0% hull your pressure hull fails and the submarine implodes — instant death. Hull repairs slowly when you are fully surfaced.
CRUSH DEPTH
As your hull weakens, the crush depth zone on the depth gauge grows upward in red. If you descend into it, the pressure will finish off your weakened hull. Always check the gauge before diving deep.
IMPLOSION WARNING
When hull reaches critical level a loud creaking sound begins and the screen flashes. You have seconds to surface and begin repairs before the hull gives way completely. Rise immediately and surface to buy time.
STATUS PANEL
The left panel shows: HULL %, DEPTH in metres, SPEED, DETECT level, SCORE, KILLS, and enemy status. The right panel mirrors hull and score in the periscope overlay. Monitor both during combat.
THE NUMBERS GAME
Hidden across every battleground are 5 numbered waypoints. Collect them all to complete a secret mission — and earn serious bonus points.
HOW TO FIND THEM
Waypoints appear as glowing numbered markers visible in periscope and command view. They are scattered across the map — some in open water, some tucked into terrain. Check the minimap: they appear as numbered blips.
HOW TO COLLECT
Navigate your submarine through the waypoint marker. When you pass through it, it is collected automatically. You must collect them in order (1 through 5). Collecting out of sequence does nothing — find them in numeric order.
SCORING
Each waypoint is worth its number × 10 points, added directly to your main score. Waypoint 1 = 10 pts, waypoint 5 = 50 pts. Collect all five and a mission accomplished message triggers. Total bonus: 150 points.
WAYPOINT PANEL
The waypoint progress panel shows which number you need next and your current waypoint score. It appears in the corner of the periscope view once the first waypoint is in range.
Good luck, Commander. The deep is unforgiving.
